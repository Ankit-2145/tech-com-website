FAQs

What is BCA?

BCA stands for Bachelor of Computer Applications. It is a 3 years degree in Computer Applications.

What are the eligibility criteria for admission into a BCA?

Candidates who have passed 10+2 examination (in any stream i.e. Science, Arts, Commerce, Vocational etc.) from H.P. Board/CBSE/ICSE or any other examination considered equivalent to 10+2, by the Himachal Pradesh University Shimla, with 40% marks (35% marks in case of SC/ST candidates).

Can I pursue higher education after completing BCA?

Yes, after completing BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications), you can pursue higher education options such as MCA (Master of Computer Applications) or other related postgraduate programs.

Will i get notes of the courses?

Yes, We will provide you with notes of your semester's courses, which will be updated daily in the evening in the Academics corner of the Resources page.

What subjects are covered in the BCA curriculum?

Programming, data structures, OS, DBMS, software eng, web dev, & computer graphics and manymore.

What is the purpose of the BCA community?

The BCA community is a platform for BCA students and professionals to connect, share resources, and collaborate on projects.

What are the benefits of joining the BCA community?

The BCA community offers many benefits, including networking, sharing resources, collaborating on projects, learning and growing, and feeling supported and connected.

Are there any events organized by the BCA community?

Yes, there are many events organized by the BCA community, such as Conferences, Meetups and Workshops These events are a great way to connect with other BCA students and professionals, learn new skills, and network.

Are there any resources shared within the BCA community?

Yes, We will provide you with all the resources you need to succeed in your career, including video lectures, projects, textbooks in PDF format, online courses, Projects Source Code and career development resources.

Do i get all source code from projects?

Yes, you will get all the source code from the projects which we will build in our live workshops. You can find it on the github link provided in the projects section of resources page.
